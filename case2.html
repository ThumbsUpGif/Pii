Research

Through research I discovered several sources of frustration across different demographics, but most interestingly I found that parents of young children could spend quite a bit of time searching for suitable playgrounds. As playgrounds can be owned and maintained by a wide range of different bodies; councils, housing associations, neighbourhood groups, schools and so on, there is no central registry in which they can be found, and more importantly from whom information about them can be comprehensively channelled to typical listing services such as Google or Four Square. That doesnâ€™t mean that playgrounds are impossible to find, after all there are plenty of them around, but itâ€™s difficult to know in advance if they are any good. According to my research, the best ways a parent could approach this matter would be to make a visual assessment from Google Mapsâ€™ satellite view or street view or, more commonly, network with other parents. This networking could be directly, through for example WhatsApp groups, or indirectly by searching online and discovering blog posts or social media comments.

Starting with a rather wide demographic I narrowed down my project by basing the app on the subgroup whose need was the most poorly served by what was currently available.
